Unit 5: Addition and Subtraction Within 100

In this unit, students review various addition and subtraction strategies, such as counting up or down and adding or subtracting ten. They learn new approaches that use place value, emphasizing both intuition and reasoning behind these strategies. To add 39 + 54, for example, students add the tens and ones separately to get 8 tens and 13 ones. Regrouping 10 ones into 1 ten gives 9 tens and 3 ones, which is 93.

Students also explore how addition and subtraction are connected. For example, they recognize that the equations 70 + ___ = 90 and 90 – 70 = ___ have the same answer; and seeing this relationship helps students think more flexibly when solving problems. Throughout the unit, they will use tools such as the 100 Chart, Base 10 Blocks, and Place Value charts to organize their thinking and deepen their understanding.

Resources
Math Tools: 100 Chart, Place Value Mat [Kinder & 1st grade only]
Students will make use of these tools to explore and solve problems.
Manipulative Tasks: Pick 'N' Plays [Kinder & 1st grade only]
Students will use dice to make two-digit numbers, one-digit numbers, and multiples of 10. Then they will add and subtract these numbers. They will also make fact families using multiples of 10.
Spiral Review (Slides, Tips and Answers) [3rd–5th grades only]
These resources help students revisit previous learned concepts throughout the year, reinforcing skills and promoting long-term retention. You may use these in addition to lessons in a way that works for your class.
Puzzles: Sum and Difference Pyramids 1, Cross-Numbers, Sum and Difference Pyramids 2, Sum Blobs, Sum and Difference Pyramids Challenge
Students will solve puzzles to practice adding and subtracting numbers.
Games: Counting Up Pig, Pig, Speed, Pick Your Pony, Addition Beeline
Students will play these games to practice strategizing, counting on the 100 chart, and adding and subtracting numbers, including mentally finding 10 more or 10 less.
